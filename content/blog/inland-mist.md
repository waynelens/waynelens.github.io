# Inland Mist

Before the street wakes up, scale becomes difficult to read. Buildings flatten into gradients and only the nearest lines stay sharp.

I used a taller aspect ratio to preserve that feeling of rising depth, where the eye travels upward and keeps searching for a horizon.

When the mist lifts, these frames lose some of their mystery, so timing becomes the main compositional tool.

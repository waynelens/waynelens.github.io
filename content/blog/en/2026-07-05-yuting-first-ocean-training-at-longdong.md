After changing jobs, Xiao Zhu finally had Sundays free for diving. I was already planning to visit Longdong that weekend to watch Yuting's first ocean training session, so I asked if he wanted to come along. It was a last-minute plan, and I had no idea who else we could travel with. Thankfully, one of Yuting's classmates offered us a ride, and the trip came together.

Their instructor had arranged an exceptionally early start: everyone had to be at Longdong by seven in the morning. The three of us living around Shuanghe called a taxi shortly before six, headed to Ximen, and transferred to our dive buddy's car. That improvised journey also led to a funny discovery—Xiao Zhu and I lived much closer to each other than either of us had realized.

My plan was to rent a buoy and weights on site while bringing my own line. When we arrived, several former students of the instructor were also there for independent practice. I had no depth target or performance goal that day. I simply entered the water with my camera to document Yuting's first ocean session and Xiao Zhu's repeated descents into the blue.

Perhaps it had something to do with the approaching typhoon, but Longdong was unusually clear that morning. The blue stretching across the surface was captivating, and sunlight reaching into the water made a familiar dive site feel entirely new. We were lucky to have such beautiful conditions for Yuting's first training day.

The ocean, however, rarely stays the same for long. During our second dive, the rising tide carried in large numbers of Portuguese man o' war. A calm practice session quickly turned into a scramble to avoid them, and we eventually made a hasty retreat to shore. The clear blue water of the morning and our chaotic exit later on somehow belonged to the same day and the same sea.

There was one last coincidence on the way home. Xiao Zhu and I mentioned Fulian Elementary School while chatting in a taxi. Our driver turned out to be a landowner from that area and told us that his family had helped donate and build the school. After a day that began before sunrise and ended with an unexpected escape from the water, it was a fitting reminder of just how small the world can be.
